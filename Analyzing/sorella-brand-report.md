# SORELLA — The Complete Brand Report
### Lore · Identity · Voice · Audit — in one document

**Prepared:** 5 July 2026
**Scope:** the definitive reference for everything Sorella is — the story it's built on, the identity it stands for, the voice it speaks in, and an honest audit of where the live store under-tells its own story.
**Sources:** full store analysis (`sorella-eg.md`, live scrape + walkthrough of sorella-eg.com), brand lore (`sorella-brand-lore.md`), voice profile (`sorella-brand-voice-profile.md`), and the working theme's deployed copy (hero, brand story, inspiration, essences, testimonials sections).

---

## Executive Summary

Sorella is a two-sister Egyptian fragrance house with a real story, a real voice, and real proof — almost none of which currently reaches the customer at the moment of purchase.

The brand's assets are unusually authentic: a founder story competitors would invent if they could (two sisters, a kitchen table, "From our bond to your senses"), a product world rooted in Egyptian daily life (perfumes named بسبوسه and ذهب, scents that smell like the Ramadan dessert table and the Sahel drive), and customers who write vivid Franco-Arabic testimonials unprompted. The catalog's price ladder — 85 LE testers to 2,890 LE bundles — is a complete trial-to-loyalty funnel in raw form.

The gap is infrastructure and articulation, not substance. The live homepage sells zero products; the sister story is exiled to an About page; the 85 LE tester (the only risk reversal in a no-returns store) is never linked from full-size product pages; five looping quotes carry the entire weight of social proof.

This report fixes the articulation half of that problem. **Part I** codifies the lore. **Part II** defines the identity. **Part III** specifies the voice — a tone system drawn entirely from Egyptian daily life, traditions, and culture, detailed to the sentence level. **Part IV** audits the distance between brand and store and ranks the fixes. **Part V** is the one-page quick reference for anyone writing in Sorella's name.

# PART I — THE LORE
*The story the brand is built on. Everything in this part is canon.*

## 1.1 The Name

**Sorella** is Italian for *sister*. One word, one fact, the whole brand: two Egyptian sisters who turned their bond into bottles.

The name is foreign-but-warm by design — it sounds like perfume, but the meaning is family. It gives the brand a European fragrance-house cadence while the *content* of the brand stays entirely Egyptian. That tension (Italian name, Egyptian soul) is not a contradiction to resolve; it is the brand's handshake: familiar enough to trust, local enough to love.

## 1.2 The Origin Story

> Two sisters. One kitchen table. A shared obsession with how a scent can hold a memory.

Sorella began with two of us blending perfume oils at a kitchen table in Egypt — no lab coats, no French house behind us, just the two people who know each other best arguing over whether the vanilla needed more salt. The first bottles went to friends. The friends came back asking for more, and asking what it was called.

It didn't have a name yet. It had a feeling: **the closeness of someone who knows you.**

That feeling became the tagline — **"From our bond to your senses"** — and the operating rule that survives to this day: every bottle is made fresh in Egypt, and every scent is a two-person decision. Nothing ships unless both sisters would wear it.

**Canonical story beats** (usable verbatim in copy, About pages, press, and packaging):
1. Sorella means sister.
2. It started at a kitchen table — it still smells like it.
3. Every scent is a two-person decision.
4. Made fresh in Egypt, always.
5. From our bond to your senses.

## 1.3 The عطّار Heritage

The craft has a lineage. Egypt has one of the world's oldest living perfume-oil traditions — the **عطّار** (attar): the neighborhood perfumer with amber jars, musk measured by the dram, and scents blended by hand and worn close to the skin.

Sorella's oils and musks are a direct continuation of that tradition — from a kitchen table instead of a shop counter. This is the brand's answer to "Made in Grasse": not a borrowed European pedigree, but an inherited Egyptian one. The عطّار heritage does three jobs at once:

- It gives the oil and musk lines a **story of provenance** (they aren't budget alternatives to sprays; they are the older, more intimate format).
- It gives the renditions line a **craft frame** ("true to the original, made the عطّار way — on rich oils, to last").
- It makes "Made fresh in Egypt" a **flex**, not a disclosure.

## 1.4 The World Sorella Lives In

Where European houses reach for lavender fields, Sorella reaches for the dessert table and the beach at Sahel. The lore in one line: **luxury perfume houses bottle Paris; Sorella bottles home** — the smells of an Egyptian summer, a Ramadan table, a sister's hug, composed seriously enough to last three days on your clothes.

The product names are the world-building:

| Product | The lore it carries |
|---|---|
| **بسبوسه (Basbousa)** | "Smells like basbousa, the Ramadan dessert we all love." A perfume named in Arabic, for a smell every Egyptian knows before they know the word *gourmand*. |
| **Baklava** | Syrup, nuts, warmth — the bestseller people get stopped on the street about. |
| **ذهب (Dahab — "gold")** | Amber richness, named the way we say it. Gold is also the brand's accent color: the name, the metal, and the palette are one motif. |
| **عود Leila** | Oud for the evening, named like a woman, not a wood. |
| **Tan Line, Coco Kiss, Sundae, Fluffy Mallow, Salted Vanilla** | The vacation shelf — "hot girl on vacation energy," sun on skin, ice cream at the beach. |
| **Wifey, Bordeaux, Classy V, Velvet Santal, Oud Royale** | The confident evening wardrobe — worn to be asked about. |

## 1.5 The Third Sister

Sorella's customers talk about the brand the way the sisters talk to each other — in Franco-Arabic, with zero PR polish:

- *"btesbat awiii"* (it really lasts)
- *"fedlet sabta tool el yoom"* (stayed put all day)
- *"lasted on el lebs for 3 days"*
- *"Bordeaux zay el original bezabt"* (exactly like the original)

This is canon: **the customer is the third sister.** Her voice is the brand's best proof material — specific performance claims, authentic dialect, even unprompted rendition validation. The testimonial section's own headline gets the doctrine right: *"Our customers tell it better than we do!"* — and the brand means it. (Part IV documents how little infrastructure currently exists to collect and display this voice; Part III sets the rule that it is never copy-edited into marketing English.)

# PART II — THE IDENTITY
*What the brand stands for, who it serves, and the lines it will not cross.*

## 2.1 Essence

**Sisterhood, bottled — Egyptian scents with world-class staying power, at prices that don't gatekeep.**

## 2.2 Positioning Statement

For young Egyptian women who love fragrance but refuse the imported-prestige markup, **Sorella** is the two-sister Egyptian fragrance house whose original gourmand and beachy scents — and faithful renditions of the world's most loved profiles — are blended fresh in Egypt on rich perfume oils, so they last for days and cost like a treat, not a tribute.

## 2.3 The Four Pillars

1. **The Bond.** Founded by two sisters; every scent a story of connection. Gifting, sharing, and "smell me!" moments are the product in use. The tagline is the first pillar spoken aloud.
2. **Egyptian Soul.** The brand's Egypt is *daily life*, not the postcard: the tea tray, the Friday lunch, basbousa after iftar, the Sahel drive, the balcony at night. Arabic names, made fresh in Egypt, spoken in the customer's own dialect — and rooted in the عطّار tradition (§1.3). Not "inspired by the Orient" — *from* here.
3. **Performance Without the Price.** Rich oil-based formulas with real longevity and sillage ("performs like bottles at four times the price"). Accessible luxury is a promise, not a euphemism: the anchor is always the prestige markup, never a discount.
4. **Honest Craft.** Three-act compositions (see §2.6), no dark patterns, honest delivery estimates, and an 85 LE tester so nobody buys an unsmelled bottle on faith alone.

## 2.4 The Two Houses of Work

Sorella's catalog tells two stories, and the identity holds both without shame:

**The Originals — the headline and the heart.** بسبوسه, Baklava, ذهب, Tan Line, Sundae… scents no one else could have made, because no one else grew up with these smells. When the brand introduces itself, this is what it leads with.

**The Renditions — the on-ramp.** "The Art of Inspiration": faithful, long-lasting reimaginings of the world's most loved fragrance profiles, built on Egyptian perfume-oil craft. Customers already say it themselves (*"zay el original bezabt"*); the brand says it with pride, not apology — **"masterfully reimagined," "true to the original"** — and never names a trademark.

**The governing rule: lead with the Originals, be honest about the Renditions.** The Originals are the lore; the Renditions are how a skeptical first-timer discovers the craft is real.

## 2.5 Audience & the Price Ladder

**Core:** Egyptian women, roughly 18–32, Instagram and TikTok native (@sorella.fragrances), fluent in Franco-Arabic, fragrance-literate through dupe culture but hungry for something that's *theirs*.
**Secondary:** gifters (sister-to-sister is the archetypal purchase) and men entering through For Him, musks, and oud.

The catalog's price ladder is a complete funnel — each rung has a name and a job:

| Rung | Price | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 5ml testers | LE 80–90 | **The handshake** — the risk-free first meeting; the trust contract of a no-returns store |
| 6ml musks | LE 300 | **The habit** — the everyday, close-to-skin repurchase |
| 8ml oils | LE 380–480 | **The ritual** — the عطّار format; intimate, concentrated, layered |
| 30–50ml EDPs | LE 640–850 | **The signature** — the scent you become known for |
| Bundles | LE 900–2,890 | **The wardrobe** — layering sets with real compare-at anchoring; every bundle clears the LE 1,100 free-gift threshold |

Everyone can afford to start. The identity's job is to make sure every rung visibly leads to the next (the audit in Part IV shows it currently doesn't).

## 2.6 The Scent Worlds

Every product belongs to one of four worlds. Each world has its own shade of the voice (detailed in Part III) and its own anchor in daily life:

| World | Members | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Dessert Table** (gourmand) | Baklava, بسبوسه, Sundae, Fluffy Mallow, Salted Vanilla, Vanille, Cherry Cream | The signature territory — cozy, indulgent, a little cheeky |
| **Sahel Summer** (beachy/solar) | Tan Line, Coco Kiss, Tropy Kiss | Vacation in a bottle — sunlit, flirty, carefree |
| **Golden Hour** (oriental/evening) | ذهب, عود Leila, Oud Royale, Velvet Santal, Bordeaux | Amber, oud, warm spice — slower, warmer, confident |
| **Second Skin** (musks & oils) | The 6ml musks and 8ml oils | Intimate and soft-spoken — the layering toolkit, worn the عطّار way |

Craft frame across all worlds: **every Sorella fragrance is composed in three acts** — the Opening (top notes), the Soul (heart notes), the Anchor (base notes). Layering is native culture, not upsell copy: bundles are sold as wardrobes ("wear them on their own or layer them together").

## 2.7 Visual & Sensory Identity

As expressed in the working theme, and to be held consistent everywhere:

- **Palette:** ink and navy dark surfaces with **gold (#C79C5A)** as the emotional accent — gold = ذهب = Dahab; the color is lore. Parchment (#F2EEE7) for text and light grounds, slate and mist as supporting neutrals. Warm and candlelit, never clinical white.
- **Texture:** soft organic "blob" glows and glass panels — scent made visible: diffuse, warm, never hard-edged.
- **Typography rhythm:** a serif display (Fraunces) with **one italic gold accent word inside each headline** ("From our *bond* to your senses," "oils and musks with *soul*"). The brand always italicizes its heart.
- **Arabic script as design element,** never a translation problem: بسبوسه on a label, ذهب as a watermark, عطّار in a craft line.
- **Motifs:** the number **two** (sisters, pairings, layering duos) and the number **three** (three acts of a composition).
- **Imagery direction:** skin, sunlight, dessert close-ups, two hands or two silhouettes; product shot warm and close, not on a white sweep.

## 2.8 Five Truths to Protect (Non-Negotiables)

1. **The sisters stay in the story.** The About-page bond must reach the purchase path — homepage, PDPs, packaging — never exiled to a footer link.
2. **Arabic names never get romanized away.** بسبوسه stays بسبوسه; pronunciation rides alongside; the script never leaves.
3. **The tester is sacred.** With a hard no-returns policy, "try it first" *is* the trust contract. It appears wherever a full bottle is sold.
4. **Customer voice stays raw.** Franco-Arabic quotes are never translated, polished, or paraphrased into marketing English.
5. **Accessible pricing is identity, not promotion.** Anchor against prestige markup; never discount the brand into "cheap."

# PART III — THE VOICE
*How Sorella sounds, sentence by sentence. The tone is drawn entirely from Egyptian daily life, traditions, and culture — written from inside the moment, never about it.*

## 3.1 The Narrator

Sorella's narrator is **your slightly-older sister who always smells incredible.** She hypes you up, lends you her perfume but watches you pump it twice max, and tells you the truth about what suits you. She is sitting next to you at the family gathering — not standing behind a Paris counter.

**Tone mix: 70% playful-warm, 30% premium. Never clinical. Never streetwear. Never a tourist in her own country.**

## 3.2 The Core Rhythm

Every piece of copy follows the same two-beat rhythm:

**Beat 1 — a lived Egyptian moment.** Open inside a scene the reader owns: a smell, a sound, a time of day from Egyptian life. Never open with a note list, and never with generic luxury imagery (silk, chandeliers, "indulgence").

**Beat 2 — the proof.** Then earn the feeling with specifics: the three-act composition, the oil-rich formula, longevity counted in hours and days, made fresh in Egypt.

Vibe first, receipts second. A hook without proof is fluff; proof without a hook is a spec sheet. Sorella is neither.

## 3.3 The Memory Bank

All hooks are drawn from — and rotated across — six territories of Egyptian daily life. This is the tone's raw material; writers should treat it as a working pantry, adding to it from lived experience.

**1. Mornings & home**
Tea with mint before anyone else wakes up. Aish baladi still warm from the bakery. Mama's kitchen the morning before a gathering. Laundry drying on the balcony in the sun. *Use for:* soft daytime scents, musks, "your everyday" copy.

**2. Ramadan & Eid**
Basbousa and konafa after iftar. The fanous glowing on the balcony. The Maghrib cannon. Kahk dust on your fingers at Eid. New clothes and eidiya. The first sip of qamar el-din. *Use for:* gourmands, seasonal campaigns (and only in season — see §3.4). Religious observance is respected context, never a sales prop.

**3. Friday & family**
The long Friday lunch. The aunts' tray of dessert that empties in minutes. Lam'et el-'eila — the family gathering. Your cousin leaning over to ask what you're wearing. *Use for:* gourmands, gifting copy, "compliment magnet" claims.

**4. Summer & Sahel**
The drive to Sahel with the playlist arguing. Salt and sunscreen on skin. Mango season. Fresca on the beach. Alexandria's corniche at sunset. "Hot girl on vacation energy." *Use for:* the beachy/solar shelf, summer campaigns.

**5. Evenings & celebration**
The Nile breeze on a felucca. Balcony tea at night. Henna night and the zaghrouta. A bride's ذهب. Grandmother's bangles and the oud burning at her house. *Use for:* Golden Hour scents, occasion and gift copy.

**6. The عطّار tradition**
Egypt's centuries-old perfume-oil craft — the attarine's amber jars, musk measured by the dram, scents blended by hand and worn close to the skin. Sorella's lineage, continued from a kitchen table instead of a shop counter. *Use for:* oils, musks, renditions, and any "why our formulas last" copy.

## 3.4 The Four Rules

1. **The insider test.** Every hook must pass: *could only someone who lives this have written it?* If a tourist brochure could say it, cut it.
2. **Specific beats general.** "Kahk dust on your fingers" beats "festive Eid sweets." One precise detail per hook is worth three vague ones.
3. **Daily beats monumental.** Sorella's Egypt is the balcony, the tea tray, the microbus window breeze — not the postcard, never the pyramids.
4. **Seasonal-honest.** Ramadan copy retires after Eid; Sahel copy runs in summer. The voice never does in prose what the live store did with "Ramadan Specials" still featured in July.

## 3.5 Language Rules

- **Bilingual by instinct.** Arabic names stay in Arabic script, glossed once on first mention: "ذهب (Dahab — 'gold')." Egyptian Arabic words season the prose naturally — kahk, konafa, fanous, eidiya, عطّار, lam'et el-'eila — lightly glossed, never footnoted into a glossary.
- **Raw proof.** Franco-Arabic customer quotes go into copy exactly as written. Never translated, never "cleaned up."
- **Anchored notes.** Fragrance notes always attach to something edible, visible, or lived: "honeyed like the first bite of basbousa," not "a gourmand semolina accord." Jargon (sillage, dry-down, accord) may appear only next to a plain-language explanation.
- **Concrete performance.** Longevity is claimed in hours on skin and days on clothes — never "guaranteed," never "100%."
- **Confident, never snobby.** Explain fragrance terms in passing to include people, not to test them.
- **One brand line per piece, maximum.** "From our bond to your senses," "composed in three acts," or "the عطّار way" — seasoning, never the meal.

## 3.6 Tone by Scent World

| World | Tone shade | Writes from |
|---|---|---|
| Dessert Table | Cozy, indulgent, a little cheeky | The aunts' dessert tray; basbousa after iftar; the first-bite argument over who made it better |
| Sahel Summer | Sunlit, flirty, carefree | The Sahel drive; salt on skin; mango season; beach fresca |
| Golden Hour | Slower, warmer, confident — evening voice | Nile breeze; henna night; bridal gold; grandmother's oud |
| Second Skin | Intimate, soft-spoken, close | The عطّار's dram bottles; scent as a private habit, applied at the wrist before going out |
| Renditions | Proud craftsman, zero apology | "True to the original, made the عطّار way — on rich oils, to last" |

## 3.7 Tone by Channel

- **Product pages:** the full four-block structure (§3.9). The most complete expression of the voice.
- **Announcement bar / banners:** one beat only — offer + warmth. "Get a free 5ml perfume over LE 1,100 — pick it in your cart."
- **Social (IG/TikTok):** narrator at her most playful; Franco-Arabic welcome in captions; memory-bank hooks as video concepts (the tray coming out of the oven, the Sahel bag being packed).
- **Email:** sister-to-sister register. Subject lines are hooks from the memory bank, not offers ("The tray is out of the oven"). Offers live in the body.
- **Packaging & inserts:** the canon beats of §1.2, one per touchpoint. The insert is the natural home of "every scent is a two-person decision."
- **Customer service:** the same older-sister honesty — plain answers, real delivery times, tester-first advice for the undecided.

## 3.8 The Lexicon

**Required phrases (and where):**

| Phrase | Placement |
|---|---|
| "Made fresh in Egypt" | Every product description (Why block) |
| "Try it first for LE 85" / "Add the 5ml tester" | CTA on every full-size EDP with a tester — the trust contract of a no-returns store |
| "Free 5ml perfume on orders over LE 1,100" | Threshold nudges: bundles, 850 LE EDPs, cart |
| "True to the original" | Renditions only. Never on Originals. |
| Concrete longevity ("stays on clothes for days") | Wherever performance is claimed |

**Forbidden words & framings (with the reason):**

| Never say | Why |
|---|---|
| dupe, copy, clone, fake, replica, "smells like [Brand X]" | Renditions are "masterfully reimagined," never named against a trademark |
| cheap, budget, discount, "affordable luxury," "luxury for less" | The identity word is *accessible*; we anchor against prestige markup, we don't discount the brand |
| pyramids, Cleopatra, "land of the pharaohs," "ancient secrets," "mysteries of the Nile," exotic, "oriental mystique," "Arabian nights" | Tourist-gaze Egypt. Sorella's Egypt is today's Egypt — the balcony, not the postcard. ("Oriental" as a plain fragrance-family term is fine.) |
| imported, "international quality" | The flex is *Made fresh in Egypt*, not elsewhere-ness |
| guaranteed, 100%, miracle, #1 | Unverifiable; performance is claimed concretely or not at all |
| "unisex fragrance for men and women" | Filler; say who it's for like a person would |
| Piety-as-marketing ("blessed") | Religious moments are respected context, never a sales prop |

## 3.9 Anatomy of a Product Description

**Four blocks, 60–90 words total:**

1. **Hook** — one sentence inside a memory-bank scene. Name the moment, not the notes.
2. **Why it works** — 2–3 features tied to benefits: three-act notes with lived anchors; oil-rich base → days-long wear; Made fresh in Egypt.
3. **Proof** — a raw Franco-Arabic or verified-buyer quote. If none exists for the product, use a concrete performance claim. Never invent a quote.
4. **CTA** — sisterly imperative + risk reversal: "Not sure yet? Try it first for LE 85."

**SEO layer:** front-load product type + key attribute ("gourmand Eau de Parfum," "vanilla perfume oil"); include the long-tail variant "long-lasting [family] perfume in Egypt"; meta description under 155 characters, always containing "Egypt," the scent family, and one benefit.

>

>
> *"It stays on me from morning until I take my jacket off at night."* — Farida M., verified buyer
>

**ذهب (Dahab) — Eau de Parfum (Golden Hour voice, 84 words):**

> **ذهب (Dahab — "gold")** is an amber Eau de Parfum for the Egyptian evening: a bride's gold catching the light, grandmother's bangles, tea poured late on the balcony.
>
> Amber, oud, and warm spice open slowly and settle close to the skin for hours — an oil-rich formula, made fresh in Egypt, built for nights that run long.
>
> *"Dahab lasted on my clothes for three days straight."* — Nour A., verified buyer
>

**The anti-example — what Sorella never sounds like:**

> ~~"Unlock the ancient secrets of the pharaohs with this exotic oriental luxury dupe. Smells 100% identical to the original designer perfume at a cheap price. Guaranteed long-lasting. Buy now!"~~

Six violations in two lines: pharaoh-postcard Egypt; "exotic oriental"; "dupe/identical"; "luxury/cheap" framing; unverifiable "100% guaranteed" — and not one real moment of Egyptian life anywhere in it.

# PART IV — THE AUDIT
*The distance between the brand in this report and the store at sorella-eg.com. The lore is real; the store under-tells it.*

## 4.1 Where the Story Leaks

Six places where the brand's best material never reaches the buyer, each tied to the identity asset it wastes:

**1. The homepage sells nothing.** A 224px scrolling text marquee for a hero; zero shoppable products on the highest-traffic page; every visitor forced through a collection page to see a single item. *Wasted asset:* the entire funnel. *Fix:* deploy the already-built richer homepage (hero with CTA → bundles bento → bestseller carousel → testimonials) — it exists in the repo and simply isn't live.

**2. The bond never reaches the purchase path.** The sister story — the brand's emotional core (§1.2) — lives only on the About page. *Wasted asset:* Pillar 1. *Fix:* brand-story section on the homepage; one canon story beat on every PDP and package insert.

**3. Five quotes and a dead link.** No review platform; five Franco-Arabic testimonials looped sitewide with a "View All" that goes nowhere; no stars on cards, PDPs, or in JSON-LD. *Wasted asset:* the third sister (§1.5) — customers already produce quotable proof unprompted. *Fix:* install a review app, seed it from existing customers, surface stars everywhere, keep the quotes raw per §3.5.

**4. The tester is invisible at the decision point.** A hard no-returns store whose LE 85 tester — its only risk reversal, and Truth #3 (§2.8) — is a separate unlinked product, frequently sold out. *Wasted asset:* the handshake rung of the ladder (§2.5). *Fix:* a "Try it first for LE 85" block on every full-size PDP; keep tester stock as a priority; reframe the returns page around the tester path.

**5. Bare prices and buried bestsellers.** No compare-at anchoring on regular PDPs (bundles prove the team can do it); collections default to alphabetical sort, surfacing "Arabian Lace" ahead of bestsellers; no badges. *Wasted asset:* Pillar 3 — "performs like bottles at four times the price" is exactly an anchoring line, unused. *Fix:* bundle-style anchoring on PDPs, best-selling default sort, Bestseller/New badges, merge size-and-tester SKUs into visible variant ladders.

**6. Ramadan in July.** Stale seasonal collections in nav and on the homepage; empty "most-loved" and "new" collections; duplicate products; `untitled-*` handles. *Wasted asset:* Honest Craft (Pillar 4) — the store's hygiene contradicts the brand's honesty. *Fix:* seasonal retirement on the calendar (the merchandising equivalent of voice rule §3.4-4), catalog cleanup, populate or remove empty collections.

*(Also on record from the store analysis, outside brand scope but blocking everything above: intermittent 503 errors burning paid traffic, and a possible sticky-ATC variant-price sync bug.)*

## 4.2 What's Already Gold

The audit is not a teardown — five things the store does that the brand should protect and extend:

1. **The cart free-gift picker** — progress bar with the exact remaining amount plus eight pre-selectable 5ml rewards, disabled until the LE 1,100 threshold. Genuinely best-in-class mechanics; none of Dossier, Thomson Carter, or PHLUR matches the combination.
2. **Sticky add-to-cart on desktop and mobile** — Dossier, at far greater scale, has none.
3. **Bundle merchandising** — real compare-at anchors, SAVE 15–25% badges, layering narratives; every bundle clears the free-gift threshold, stacking incentives.
4. **An honest shop** — no pre-selected memberships, no popup spam, truthful delivery estimates, solid mobile UX.
5. **The voice itself** — the sister story, Arabic names, and vivid Franco-Arabic testimonials are proof material competitors would pay for. The raw asset is real; only the infrastructure is missing.

## 4.3 Priority Order

Ranked by expected impact, brand-consistency preserved throughout:

1. Ship the built-but-undeployed homepage (leak #1) — the lore finally meets the traffic.
2. Tester cross-link block on every full-size PDP (leak #4) — Truth #3 made visible.
3. Review app installed and seeded (leak #3) — the third sister gets a stage.
4. PDP anchoring + best-selling sort + badges (leak #5).
5. Brand-story section into the purchase path (leak #2).
6. Seasonal and catalog hygiene pass (leak #6).
7. Rewrite all product descriptions to the Part III system — the description rewriter skill plus the voice profile are ready; bestsellers first.

Sorella means sister: two Egyptian sisters who turned a kitchen table into a fragrance house. It bottles the Egypt its customers actually live — the dessert table, the Sahel drive, the henna night — on oil-rich formulas that last for days, at prices that don't gatekeep.
