## 1. The Lore — the story we tell

### The name

**Sorella** is Italian for *sister*. One word, one fact, the whole brand: two Egyptian sisters who turned their bond into bottles. The name is foreign-but-warm on purpose — it sounds like perfume, but the meaning is family.

### The origin story

> Two sisters. One kitchen table. A shared obsession with how a scent can hold a memory.

Sorella began with two of us blending perfume oils at a kitchen table in Egypt — no lab coats, no French house behind us, just the two people who know each other best arguing over whether the vanilla needed more salt. The first bottles went to friends. The friends came back asking for more, and asking what it was called. It didn't have a name yet. It had a feeling: the closeness of someone who knows you.

That feeling became the tagline: **"From our bond to your senses."** Every bottle is still made fresh in Egypt, and every scent is still a two-person decision — nothing ships unless both sisters would wear it.

### The world Sorella lives in

Sorella's scent memory is Egyptian, and unapologetically so. Where European houses reach for lavender fields, Sorella reaches for the dessert table and the beach at Sahel:

- **بسبوسه (Basbousa)** — "smells like basbousa, the Ramadan dessert we all love." A perfume named in Arabic, for a smell every Egyptian knows before they know the word *gourmand*.
- **Baklava** — syrup, nuts, warmth; the bestseller people get stopped on the street about.
- **ذهب (Dahab)** — "gold": amber richness with the name written the way we say it.
- **عود Leila** — oud for the evening, named like a woman, not a wood.
- **Tan Line, Coco Kiss, Sundae, Fluffy Mallow, Salted Vanilla** — the vacation shelf: "hot girl on vacation energy," sun on skin, ice cream in Marassi.

The lore in one line: **luxury perfume houses bottle Paris; Sorella bottles home.** The smells of an Egyptian summer, a Ramadan table, a sister's hug — composed seriously enough to last three days on your clothes.

### The proof is the sisterhood

Sorella's customers talk about it the way the sisters talk to each other — in Franco-Arabic, with zero PR polish: *"btesbat awiii," "fedlet sabta tool el yoom," "lasted on el lebs for 3 days."* The brand's social proof isn't a press bar; it's a group chat. That is canon: **customers are the third sister.** The testimonial section is titled "Our customers tell it better than we do!" — and the brand means it.

## 2. Brand Identity

### Essence (one line)

**Sisterhood, bottled — Egyptian scents with world-class staying power, at prices that don't gatekeep.**

### Positioning statement

For young Egyptian women who love fragrance but refuse the imported-prestige markup, Sorella is the two-sister Egyptian fragrance house whose original gourmand and beachy scents — and faithful renditions of the world's most loved profiles — are blended fresh in Egypt on rich perfume oils, so they last for days and cost like a treat, not a tribute.

### The four pillars

1. **The Bond.** Founded by two sisters; every scent a story of connection. Gifting, sharing, and "smell me!" moments are the product in use. ("From our bond to your senses.")
2. **Egyptian Soul.** The brand's Egypt is *daily life*, not the postcard: the tea tray, the Friday lunch, basbousa after iftar, the Sahel drive, the balcony at night. Arabic names, made fresh in Egypt, spoken in the customer's own dialect — and rooted in the عطّار (attar) tradition, Egypt's centuries-old perfume-oil craft that Sorella continues from a kitchen table instead of a shop counter. Not "inspired by the Orient" — *from* here.
3. **Performance Without the Price.** Rich oil-based formulas with real longevity and sillage ("performs like bottles at four times the price"). Accessible luxury is a promise, not a euphemism.
4. **Honest Craft.** Three-act compositions (Opening / Soul / Anchor), no dark patterns, honest delivery estimates, an 85 LE tester so you can try before you commit.

### The two houses of work

Sorella's catalog tells two stories, and the identity holds both without shame:

- **The Originals** — Basbousa, Baklava, Dahab, Tan Line, Sundae… scents no one else could have made, because no one else grew up with these smells. This is the brand's heart and its headline.
- **The Renditions** — "The Art of Inspiration": faithful, long-lasting reimaginings of the world's most loved fragrance profiles ("True to the Original"), built on Egyptian perfume-oil craft. Customers already say it themselves — *"zay el original bezabt."* The brand says it with pride, not apology: *masterfully reimagined.*

Rule of thumb: **lead with the Originals, be honest about the Renditions.** The Originals are the lore; the Renditions are the on-ramp.

### Audience

- **Core:** Egyptian women, ~18–32, Instagram + TikTok native (@sorella.fragrances), fluent in Franco-Arabic, fragrance-literate through dupe culture but hungry for something that's *theirs*.
- **Secondary:** gifters (sister-to-sister is the archetypal purchase), and men entering through For Him / musks / oud.
- **Entry price psychology:** the ladder is the funnel — 85 LE tester → 300 LE musk → 380 LE oil → 640–850 LE EDP → 900–2,890 LE bundle. Everyone can afford to start.

### Personality & voice

**If Sorella were a person:** your slightly-older sister who always smells incredible, lends you her perfume but watches you pump it twice max, and tells you the truth about what suits you.

- **Warm, not precious.** "Smells like basbousa" beats "a gourmand accord of semolina and honey."
- **Bilingual by instinct.** Arabic names stay in Arabic script (بسبوسه, ذهب). Franco-Arabic appears in proof and social, English in structure. Never translate the soul out of a name.
- **Playful with receipts.** Vibe-first hooks ("hot girl on vacation energy") backed by concrete performance claims (three days on clothes, sillage hours).
- **Confident, never snobby.** We explain notes ("Olfactory Architecture") to include people, not to test them.
- **Honest to a fault.** Real delivery times, real prices, try-before-you-buy testers. If we can't say it straight, we don't say it.

**Say / don't say:**

| Say | Don't say |
|---|---|
| "Made fresh in Egypt" | "Imported quality" |
| "From our bond to your senses" | Generic "luxury for less" |
| "Smells like the Ramadan dessert we all love" | Note-pyramid jargon with no anchor |
| "Try it first for LE 85" | "All sales final" energy |
| "Masterfully reimagined" / "True to the Original" | "Dupe," "copy," "fake" |

### Scent-world map (how the catalog clusters)

- **Dessert Table (gourmand):** Basbousa, Baklava, Sundae, Fluffy Mallow, Salted Vanilla, Vanille, Cherry Cream — the signature territory.
- **Sahel Summer (beachy/solar):** Tan Line, Coco Kiss, Tropy Kiss — vacation in a bottle.
- **Golden Hour (oriental/evening):** Dahab, عود Leila, Oud Royale, Velvet Santal, Bordeaux — amber, oud, warm spice; "our richest compositions for evenings that linger."
- **Second Skin (musks & oils):** the 6ml musks and 8ml oils — "intimate scents that stay close to the skin," the layering toolkit.

Layering is native culture, not upsell copy: bundles are sold as wardrobes ("wear them on their own or layer them together," "a scent for every mood").

### Verbal identity — the messaging bank

- **Tagline:** From our bond to your senses.
- **Origin line:** Sorella means sister. It started at a kitchen table — it still smells like it.
- **Craft line:** Every Sorella fragrance is composed in three acts: the Opening, the Soul, the Anchor.
- **Value line:** Premium quality without the prestige markup.
- **Renditions line:** The world's most loved profiles, masterfully reimagined — true to the original, made to last for days.
- **Proof line:** Our customers tell it better than we do.
- **Risk-reversal line:** Try it first for LE 85.
- **Gift line:** A gift of scent — from sisters, to your story.

### Visual & sensory identity (as expressed in the working theme)

- **Palette:** ink-dark surfaces with **gold** as the emotional accent (gold = ذهب = Dahab — the color is lore), softened by slate; warm, candlelit rather than clinical white.
- **Texture:** soft organic "blob" glows and glass panels — scent made visible: diffuse, warm, never hard-edged.
- **Typography rhythm:** an accent word set apart inside each headline ("From our **bond** to your senses," "with **soul**," "the **oriental** evening edit") — the brand always italicizes its heart.
- **Imagery direction:** skin, sunlight, dessert close-ups, two hands / two silhouettes; product shot warm and close, not on white sweep.
- **Motifs:** the number two (sisters, pairings, layering duos); three acts (notes pyramid); Arabic script as design element, not translation problem.

### Brand truths to protect (non-negotiables)

1. The sisters stay in the story — the About page bond must reach the purchase path, not live in exile.
2. Arabic names never get romanized away.
3. The tester is sacred: with no returns, "try it first" *is* the trust contract.
4. Customer voice stays raw — Franco-Arabic quotes don't get copy-edited into English marketing speak.
5. Accessible pricing is identity, not promotion — anchor against prestige, never discount the brand into "cheap."
